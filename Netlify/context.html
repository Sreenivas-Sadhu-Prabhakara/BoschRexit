Context & Challenge

Understanding the "Hidden" Pain Points in Industrial Automation

Strategic Context

Bosch Rexroth is a global leader in drive and control technologies. For decades, the brand was synonymous with "Heavy Iron"—indestructible hydraulics and mechanical engineering.

However, by 2018, the industrial automation market was shifting. Hardware was becoming commoditized. The value was moving to software, connectivity, and intelligence.

The company faced a critical crossroads: Continue selling "dumb" but high-quality components, or risk disrupting their own business model to become a digital player.

1. Market Pressure (2018)

The manufacturing landscape was under severe stress. While competitors like Siemens, ABB, and Rockwell pushed modular systems, customers struggled with implementation.

The "Hidden" Pain Points

Internal analysis revealed structural customer pain that purely technical innovation couldn't fix:

Customer Type Pain Point
OEMs (Machine Builders) Integration Fatigue: Hiring software engineers to code simple mechanical movements was destroying their margins.
End Users (Factories) Skills Gap: Maintenance teams couldn't fix complex "black box" systems, leading to costly downtime.

2. The Challenge

Launching without deep insight risked a classic "Engineering Trap"—building what is technically possible rather than what is operationally necessary.

The Key Strategic Question

"Did customers want smarter machines (AI/ML) — or did they just want faster speed-to-revenue?"
